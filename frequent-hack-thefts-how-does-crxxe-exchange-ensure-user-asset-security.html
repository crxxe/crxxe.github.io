The Threat of Hack Attacks in the Cryptocurrency Industry

In recent years, hack attacks by the Lazarus Group have become commonplace. According to a report by the UN Security Council Sanctions Committee, there have been 97 suspected North Korean hacker attacks on cryptocurrency companies from 2017 to 2024, totalling approximately $3.6 billion. These attacks have not only caused significant financial losses to the affected exchanges but have also severely undermined trust and stability in the cryptocurrency industry.

A notable example is the $147.5 million theft from HTX cryptocurrency exchange. This incident occurred late last year when hackers successfully stole funds through a meticulously planned cyberattack and laundered them via Tornado Cash in March this year. Despite the US imposing sanctions on Tornado Cash in 2022 and charging its co-founders in 2023, laundering activities remain rampant.

Additionally, according to an investigation by cryptocurrency detective ZachXBT, the Lazarus Group laundered $200 million worth of cryptocurrency into fiat currency between August 2020 and October 2023 using various methods. They exploited social engineering and phishing attacks to trick victims into downloading malware and obtain sensitive information. For example, they posted fake job ads on LinkedIn to lure employees of military and aerospace companies in Europe and the Middle East into downloading malicious PDF files.

Similar tactics were used to attack cryptocurrency payment provider CoinsPaid. During a six-month operation, the Lazarus Group sent fake job offers to CoinsPaid engineers and launched distributed denial-of-service attacks and brute force attacks, resulting in a $37 million loss for CoinsPaid. These attacks are not only complex and varied but also target a wide range of entities, from cryptocurrency exchanges to payment providers, sparing no one.
